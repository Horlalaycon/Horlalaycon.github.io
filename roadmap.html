Fundamental Knowledges

Here are some fundamental Knownledges that a hacker should possess.

user@localhost:~$

Computer Networking
  • Understanding of TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, and other network protocols
  • Familiarity with network architectures, including LAN, WAN, and Wi-Fi
  • Knowledge of network devices, such as routers, switches, and firewalls
user@localhost:~$

Operating system
  • Understanding of Windows, Linux, and macOS operating systems
  • Familiarity with command-line interfaces, such as Bash and PowerShell
  • Knowledge of file systems, permissions, and access control
user@localhost:~$

Programming
  • Understanding of programming languages, such as Python, C++, and Java
  • Familiarity with scripting languages, such as Bash and Perl
  • Knowledge of data structures, algorithms, and software development life cycles
user@localhost:~$

Cryptography
  • Understanding of encryption algorithms, such as AES and RSA
  • Familiarity with hashing algorithms, such as MD5 and SHA
  • Knowledge of digital signatures, certificates, and public key infrastructure (PKI)
user@localhost:~$

Web Application
  • Understanding of HTTP, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Familiarity with web application frameworks, such as PHP and Ruby on Rails
  • Knowledge of web application security, including SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS)
user@localhost:~$

Database
  • Understanding of database management systems, such as MySQL and Oracle
  • Familiarity with database structures, including tables, indexes, and queries
  • Knowledge of database security, including authentication and access control
user@localhost:~$

Social Engineering Fundamentals
  • Understanding of human psychology and behavior
  • Familiarity with social engineering tactics, such as phishing and pretexting
  • Knowledge of how to identify and resist social engineering attacks
user@localhost:~$

Vulnerability Identification and Exploitation
  • Understanding of vulnerability scanning and penetration testing
  • Familiarity with exploit development and vulnerability exploitation
  • Knowledge of how to identify and prioritize vulnerabilities
user@localhost:~$

Post-Exploitation and Lateral Movement
  • Understanding of post-exploitation techniques, such as privilege escalation and persistence
  • Familiarity with lateral movement techniques, such as network mapping and credential harvesting
  • Knowledge of how to maintain access and move laterally within a network
user@localhost:~$

Evading Detection and Evasion
  • Understanding of detection mechanisms, such as intrusion detection systems (IDS) and antivirus software
  • Familiarity with evasion techniques, such as code obfuscation and anti-forensic techniques
  • Knowledge of how to evade detection and remain stealthy during an attack
user@localhost:~$

Reverse Engineering and Malware Analysis
  • Understanding of reverse engineering techniques, such as disassembly and debugging
  • Familiarity with malware analysis, including static and dynamic analysis
  • Knowledge of how to analyze and understand malware behavior
user@localhost:~$

Incident Response and Threat Hunting
  • Understanding of incident response methodologies, such as NIST and SANS
  • Familiarity with threat hunting techniques, such as network traffic analysis and endpoint detection
  • Knowledge of how to respond to and contain security incidents
user@localhost:~$

Conclusion

Ultimately, the most important thing for a hacker is to be curious and to have a desire to learn. However, it is important to note that not all hackers possess all of these skills.
